My Roles

Host, Busser, and All Kitchen Positions

My Responsibilities

Host:

  • Answered phone calls, and made reservations
  • Coordinated with servers and table layout to ensure guests were distributed evenly and sat in a timely manner
  • Interacted with the guests during the walk to the table and accommodated any request they might have had
  • Kept guests company if they were waiting up front on their way out

Busser:

  • Made sure that tables were cleared, cleaned, and set for the next party shortly after the guests left
  • Helped servers meet their table's needs and keep guests satisfied
  • Regularly checked on the dining room for anything that in need of cleaning or guests needing assistance
  • Communicated with the hosts via headset so everyone had the latest updates on the dining room

Kitchen:

  • Worked fry cook, assembler, dishwasher, sauté, baker, food prep, and broiler
  • Maintained a clean, organized, and fully stocked station
  • Worked seamlessly with coworkers to get dishes out cooked and plated to perfection
  • Made use of downtime by making sure others were caught up

About Red Lobster

Before there was Red Lobster, there was Bill Darden, a man passionate about making delicious, high-quality seafood available and affordable to everyone, including people who lived far from the coast and regardless of race, gender, religion or economic means. What was once a single, family-owned restaurant in Lakeland, Florida, now has over 700 locations around the world.
